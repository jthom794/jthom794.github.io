Christ the Redeemer

Location: Christ the Redeemer (Cristo Redentor in Portuguese) is located on the summit of the Corcovado mountain in the Tijuca Forest National Park, overlooking the city of Rio de Janeiro.

Statue: The statue is a colossal representation of Jesus Christ, standing with outstretched arms. It measures approximately 98 feet (30 meters) in height, including its pedestal, making it one of the largest Art Deco statues in the world.

Construction: The statue was designed by Brazilian engineer Heitor da Silva Costa and created by French sculptor Paul Landowski. Construction began in 1922 and was completed in 1931. The statue is made of reinforced concrete and soapstone.

Symbolism: Christ the Redeemer is a symbol of Christianity and a representation of Jesus as the Savior of the world. It is also a symbol of peace, welcoming visitors to Rio de Janeiro with open arms.

UNESCO World Heritage Site: In 2012, Christ the Redeemer and the surrounding Tijuca Forest National Park were designated as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in recognition of their cultural and natural significance.

Tourist Attraction: The statue and the panoramic views from the Corcovado mountain are a major tourist attraction in Rio de Janeiro and Brazil as a whole. Visitors can reach the summit by hiking, taking a train, or driving.

Cultural Icon: Christ the Redeemer has become an enduring cultural symbol of Brazil and is featured in numerous films, TV shows, and advertisements. It is a popular backdrop for events, including New Year's Eve celebrations.

Conservation and Restoration: The statue has undergone multiple restoration efforts over the years to preserve its integrity. Conservation work has been necessary due to weathering and environmental factors.

Lighting: The statue is often illuminated at night, and its lighting is sometimes used to mark special occasions or events. Various lighting displays have enhanced its visual impact.
